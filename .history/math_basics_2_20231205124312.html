Self-Similarity: Fractals have a self-replicating pattern, which means they contain smaller copies of themselves within the larger structure.

Infinite Detail: No matter how closely you zoom in, you'll always find more intricate patterns and shapes.

Complexity: Despite having simple mathematical rules, fractals can exhibit extremely complex and intricate shapes.

Natural and Mathematical: Fractals can be found in nature (such as in the branching of trees, coastlines, or snowflakes) and are also generated mathematically using iterative processes or recursive algorithms.

A fractal is a complex geometric shape that can be divided into parts, each of which is a reduced-scale copy of the whole.
